YNC Unofficial Module Brief - AY21/22 Sem 2

YSS3268: Anthropology of China

Anthropology • Chinese Studies • 5 MCs

Instructors
Zachary Howlett

No exam

Prerequisites
YCC2121 Modern Social Thought or with the permission of the instructor

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 3, Project: 3, Prep: 6.5)

Schedule
E1: Tue 10:30 - 12:00, Fri 10:30 - 12:00 (Y-PgRm2)

The rise of China is creating unprecedented global challenges and opportunities. This course helps students achieve a nuanced cultural understanding of this potential superpower by critically examining the concepts of “China” and “Chineseness” from an anthropological perspective. Topics include ethnic relations, imperialism, and the civilized-barbarian distinction; gender, patriarchy, and the family; popular religion, popular culture, and rebellion; bureaucracy, corruption, and social connections (guanxi); and overseas Chinese and the Chinese diasporas. In addition to reading classic and contemporary works of China anthropology, students will watch some highly selected films and documentaries on China. No knowledge of the Chinese language is required.

YLC3203: Advanced Chinese 1

Chinese • Chinese Studies • 5 MCs

Instructors
Zhang Mo

Exam
04 May 2022 09:00 - 12:00

Prerequisites
YLC2202 Intermediate Chinese 2 or passed GCE Chinese O & A Levels or with the permission of the instructor.

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 4, Project: 2, Prep: 6.5)

Schedule
E1: Tue 14:30 - 16:00, Thu 14:30 - 15:30, Fri 14:30 - 16:00 (Y-CR18)

This course, together with Advanced Chinese 2, bridges the gap between our current offerings of intermediate and advanced-level Chinese, serving students who have completed YLC 2202 Intermediate Chinese 2 or equivalent. It continues to develop students’ abilities in speaking, orally comprehending, reading, and writing modern Mandarin Chinese and, concomitantly, also serves to deepen students’ understanding of the social and cultural issues facing China today. Students will be routinely drilled in reading and writing throughout the semester. Systematic discussion, debate, and presentation provide students with ample opportunities to practice and enhance their newly acquired linguistic skills and oral fluency.

YSS3315: The Chinese Economy

Economics • Chinese Studies • Global Affairs • 5 MCs

Instructors
Pei Gao

No exam

Prerequisites
A-level Economics (or equivalent) or YSS1203 Principles of Economics or with the permission of the instructor. Intermediate Macroeconomics and knowledge of basic statistics is NOT required but would be an asset.

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 3, Prep: 9.5)

Schedule
E1: Mon 18:00 - 19:30, Thu 18:00 - 19:30 (Y-PgRm1)

What are the fundamental causes of modern economic development? This module anchors the answers to this question using the evolution of China’s economic development as a case study. It surveys the major research topics in Chinese economy: state (political selection, corruption for example), firms (SOEs, trade) and households (fertility, inequality). The period covered extends from the imperial period to today (the pre-modern, communist, and reform periods). This module will familiarize students with issues related to the Chinese economy: its history, the success of its reforms and the reasons for this success, and challenges it is facing.

YSS3212: Chinese Politics

Global Affairs • PPE • Chinese Studies • 5 MCs

Instructors
Steven Matthew Oliver

No exam

Prerequisites
YCC2121 Modern Social Thought or with the permission of the instructor.

Corequisites
YCC2121 Modern Social Thought or with the permission of the instructor.

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 3, Prep: 9.5)

Schedule
E1: Mon 18:00 - 19:30, Thu 18:00 - 19:30 (ERC-SR9CAM)

China’s rise is arguably one of the most important features of the 21st Century. Its growth-driven model of single-party rule challenges democratic ideals nurtured since WWII and its growing international economic and political clout promises to transform the existing regional and international orders. How did China get to this point? Where is it going? This class explores China’s political and economic development beginning from the early Republican era, charts the Chinese Revolution, then turns to the Maoist and Reform eras in which it explores China’s transition from a closed and impoverished agriculture society to regional and world power.

YHU2212: Classical Chinese

Literature • Chinese Studies • Chinese • Global Antiquity • 5 MCs

Instructors
Scott Cook

Exam
27 Apr 2022 13:00 - 16:00

No prerequisites

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 4, Project: 4, Prep: 4.5)

Schedule
E1: Mon 16:00 - 18:00, Thu 16:00 - 18:00 (Y-GLRm2)

This course will introduce students to the basic particles and grammatical structure of the classical Chinese language (a.k.a. literary Chinese). Through the close reading of texts from the pre- and early-imperial periods, students will also learn such skills as recognizing syntactic parallelism, the art of reading in context, and understanding rhetorical structures.

YHU3289: Beyond China: Sinophone Literature, Film, and Culture

Literature • Chinese Studies • 5 MCs

Instructors
Ma Shao-Ling

No exam

Prerequisites
YCC1111 Literature and Humanities 1 and YCC1112 Literature and Humanities 2 or with the permission of the instructor.

Workload
12.5 hours (Tutorial: 3, Project: 2.5, Prep: 7)

Schedule
E1: Tue 16:00 - 17:30, Fri 16:00 - 17:30 (Y-PgRm2)

“China” and “Chinese” are no longer adequate terms for the study of Sinitic-language communities and cultures that evince politically tenuous and linguistically polyphonic relations with the People’s Republic of China. This course interrogates Sinophone Studies as a critical, interdisciplinary alternative to previous constructions of Chinese literature and culture tied to the nation-state and progressive narratives of modernity. Students will study fiction and films from Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, Taiwan, the United States, and the PRC, along with critical works explaining the ways that diaspora, colonialism, comparative empires, and ethnic or minority studies have informed the rise of Sinophone Studies. All Chinese-language materials will be read in English translation.
